Privacy Policy

Last updated: 29 June 2026

The short version: Spark is a private, on-device journal. Your entries, moods, photos, and notes are stored only on your phone. We don't have accounts, we don't run servers that store your journal, we don't use analytics or ads, and we never sell your data. The only information that ever leaves your device is what's needed to process a purchase, and — if you choose to dictate — the audio handled by your phone's own speech recognition.

This policy explains what Spark ("the app", "we", "us") does with information when you use it. It applies to the Spark mobile app on iOS and Android.

1. Who we are

Spark is published by Quiet Spark Studio. If you have any questions about this policy or your privacy, contact us at quietsparkstudio5@gmail.com.

2. Data stored on your device

Everything you create in Spark is saved locally on your device (in the app's private storage) and is not uploaded to us:

  • Your journal entries (the sentence you write each day)
  • Your selected mood for each entry
  • Photos you attach to entries
  • Tags you add to entries
  • Voice transcriptions you create (the resulting text)
  • App settings, your streak, and preferences (theme, reminder time, etc.)

Because this data lives only on your device, we cannot see it, access it, or recover it for you. It is protected by your device's own security and, optionally, by the in-app biometric lock.

3. What we do not collect

  • No account, email, name, or sign-up required.
  • No advertising and no ad identifiers.
  • No third-party analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • No location tracking.
  • We never sell or rent your personal information to anyone.

4. When information leaves your device

a) Purchases (Spark Pro)

If you buy a Spark Pro subscription, the purchase is processed by the Apple App Store or Google Play, and we use RevenueCat to verify and manage your subscription status. For this, a randomly generated, anonymous app user ID and your purchase/receipt information are shared with RevenueCat and the app store. This does not include your journal content. See:

  • RevenueCat Privacy Policy
  • Apple Privacy Policy / Google Privacy Policy

b) Voice dictation (optional)

If you use voice-to-text to write an entry, your spoken audio is processed by your device's built-in speech recognition service (provided by Apple on iOS or Google on Android). Depending on your device settings, this audio may be sent to Apple or Google to be transcribed, under their respective privacy policies. Spark only receives the resulting text. If you prefer not to use this, simply don't tap the microphone.

c) Things you choose to share

Spark lets you share a "Spark Wrapped" image or export your entries as a file. These actions only happen when you initiate them, and the content goes wherever you choose to send it (Messages, email, etc.). We are not involved in that transfer.

5. Notifications

If you enable daily reminders, they are scheduled and delivered locally on your device. We do not run a push-notification server and we do not see whether or when reminders fire.

6. Permissions we request

  • Photos / Camera — only to let you attach images to an entry.
  • Microphone & Speech Recognition — only when you use voice dictation.
  • Notifications — only to send the daily reminder you opted into.
  • Face ID / Fingerprint — only to unlock the app if you turn on the privacy lock. Biometric data never leaves your device and is never seen by Spark.

You can change or revoke any of these permissions at any time in your device settings.

7. Children's privacy

Spark is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your country), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

8. Keeping and deleting your data

You're always in control:

  • Delete entries: use "Delete all entries" in Settings to permanently remove your journal.
  • Export: use "Export my data" to save a copy of your entries as a file.
  • Uninstall: removing the app deletes all of its on-device data.

Subscription records held by the app store / RevenueCat are retained according to their policies.

9. Security

Your data is stored in the app's private, sandboxed storage on your device and can be additionally protected with the in-app biometric lock. No system is perfectly secure, but because your journal never leaves your device, it is never exposed to a server breach.

10. Your rights

Depending on where you live (for example under GDPR or CCPA), you may have rights to access, correct, or delete your personal data. Because Spark stores your content only on your device, you already have direct and complete control over it through the app. For anything related to purchase records, contact us at quietsparkstudio5@gmail.com.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We'll revise the "Last updated" date above, and significant changes will be reflected in the app or store listing.

12. Contact

Questions? Reach us at quietsparkstudio5@gmail.com.
